EDITORIAL

Illustration for magazines and publications. Making complex ideas visual, accessible, and beautiful.

MAKE LEARNING FUN

This piece celebrates the messy, colorful, unpredictable joy of learning. Children surrounded by books, experiments, and creative chaos — a visual argument that education works best when it feels like play.

The composition deliberately avoids the sterile order of a typical classroom, presenting learning as an organic, explosive process. Colors spill across boundaries, objects overlap, scale shifts playfully.

"The hardest part is finding the visual metaphor," Ding Ding notes. "Once you have that, the rest follows."

Published in: Various magazines · Medium: Digital illustration

DESIGN FOR A BETTER WORLD

A visual exploration of how design thinking can address social challenges — from education access to environmental sustainability. The illustration combines data visualization with Ding Ding's signature vibrant style.

The compositional strategy uses architecture as metaphor: buildings and structures become vessels for human aspiration, each window revealing a different dimension of "better." The "better world" depicted is not utopian — it's messy, under construction, full of scaffolding.

Her editorial clients include VOGUE, Wallpaper*, and New Weekly (新周刊) — publications that value illustration as a form of visual thinking.
