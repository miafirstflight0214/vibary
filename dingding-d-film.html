YOLO 热辣滚烫

Film Poster · 2024
Poster for "YOLO 热辣滚烫" (2024), directed by Jia Ling.

This poster captures the raw emotional transformation at the heart of Jia Ling's 2024 film. The composition layers everyday life — food stalls, gym equipment, boxing gloves — into a vibrant tapestry that mirrors the protagonist's journey from passivity to self-determination.

The color palette shifts from warm, comfortable tones at the bottom to intense, electric hues at the top, echoing the film's narrative arc. Every detail rewards close inspection: hidden faces, symbolic objects, visual rhymes between foreground and background.

Ding Ding's approach to film poster illustration rejects the conventional photographic key art that dominates Chinese cinema marketing. Instead, she builds an entire world within the frame — one that captures not just the plot, but the feeling of watching the film.

"A poster should make you feel something before you know anything about the story," she explains. "It's not a summary. It's an invitation."

The YOLO poster became one of the most recognized pieces of film art in 2024, widely shared on social media and praised for its ability to condense a two-hour emotional journey into a single, endlessly rewarding image.

Client: Lu Films 路画影视
Year: 2024
Medium: Digital illustration

B for Busy 爱情神话

Film Poster · 2021

Shanghai's quiet romance rendered through the lens of urban life. Window views, street corners, the beautiful mundane — a city breathing in illustration.

The poster for "B for Busy" (爱情神话) captures the film's spirit of accidental beauty and understated romance. Unlike the explosive energy of YOLO, this piece whispers. The viewer discovers the story gradually: a glance through a window, a bicycle leaning against a plane tree, laundry drying on a balcony.

The composition uses Shanghai's distinctive architectural vocabulary — French Concession lanes, corner cafés, aging art deco facades — as both setting and character. The city itself becomes the protagonist.

"B for Busy 爱情神话" — Shanghai's everyday magic, rendered in layers.

The color treatment is notably restrained for Ding Ding's work — warm ochres, muted greens, the particular gray-blue of Shanghai winter light. This restraint serves the film's tone: romantic but never sentimental, warm but never saccharine.

Year: 2021
Medium: Digital illustration
