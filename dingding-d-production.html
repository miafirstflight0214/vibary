GO TO Museum — Coloring Sets

Production Design · Package Design
GO TO Museum Coloring Sets — inviting children into the world of art museums.

These coloring sets bridge the gap between illustration and product design. Each set takes children on a guided tour through a famous museum, with Ding Ding's illustrations serving as both artwork and activity.

The challenge was creating illustrations that work at multiple levels: beautiful enough to frame as prints, yet designed to invite active participation through coloring, tracing, and creative reinterpretation.

The packaging design reflects the museum experience itself — the boxes are designed to feel like miniature exhibition catalogs, with careful attention to paper stock, printing technique, and unboxing sequence.

"Children notice quality," Ding Ding explains. "If you give them something that feels precious, they treat it preciously. The packaging is part of the education."

The China edition features domestic museums (National Museum, Palace Museum, Shanghai Museum), while the overseas edition adapts the concept for international collections.

Client: Museum partnership
Medium: Illustration + package design
Format: Coloring sets, two editions

Overseas Edition

Production Design

Two editions, two markets, one vision: making museum culture accessible and delightful for young explorers everywhere.

The overseas edition required rethinking not just which museums to feature, but how to communicate the concept of "museum visit" to children in different cultural contexts. The illustrations had to be universally legible while retaining Ding Ding's distinctive visual voice.
