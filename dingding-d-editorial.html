Make Learning Fun

Editorial Illustration
Editorial illustration exploring how play and curiosity transform education.

This piece celebrates the messy, colorful, unpredictable joy of learning. Children surrounded by books, experiments, and creative chaos — a visual argument that education works best when it feels like play.

The composition deliberately avoids the sterile order of a typical classroom, instead presenting learning as an organic, explosive process. Colors spill across boundaries, objects overlap, scale shifts playfully.

Ding Ding's editorial work brings the same narrative density she applies to film posters, but with a different challenge: making abstract ideas tangible. Where a film poster has a story to anchor it, an editorial illustration must create its own world from a concept alone.

"The hardest part is finding the visual metaphor," she notes. "Once you have that, the rest follows."

Her editorial clients include VOGUE, Wallpaper*, and New Weekly (新周刊) — publications that value illustration not as decoration, but as a form of visual thinking.

Published in: Various magazines

Design for a Better World

Editorial Illustration

A visual exploration of how design thinking can address social challenges — from education access to environmental sustainability. The illustration combines data visualization with Ding Ding's signature vibrant style, creating a piece that is both informative and emotionally engaging.

The compositional strategy uses architecture as metaphor: buildings and structures become vessels for human aspiration, each window revealing a different dimension of "better."

"Design for a Better World" — architecture as metaphor for human aspiration.

What distinguishes this work is its optimism without naïveté. The "better world" depicted is not utopian — it's messy, under construction, full of scaffolding. It's a world being actively built by visible hands.
