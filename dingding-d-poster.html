Smart-59

Poster Design
Smart-59: Bold graphic poster capturing Beijing's rapid modernization and cultural collisions.

A bold graphic poster that captures Beijing's rapid modernization — the tension between old hutongs and glass towers, between tradition and disruption. The "59" is both address and countdown: a city reinventing itself at speed.

The poster uses isometric perspective to create a sense of simultaneous overview and intimacy. You see the whole city, but you also see individual lives playing out in each window, on each rooftop.

Ding Ding's poster work distills complex urban narratives into single, impactful compositions. Each poster is designed to stop you in motion — on a subway platform, walking past a construction wall, scrolling through a feed.

"A poster has about half a second to grab you," she says. "Everything — color, composition, typography — has to serve that half-second. But then it has to reward the second look, the third look, the hundredth look."

This dual mandate — instant impact plus sustained depth — is the signature tension of her poster work.

Medium: Digital illustration
Format: Print poster

Wangjing Panda 望京熊猫

Poster Design

Beijing's Wangjing district reimagined through the eyes of a wandering panda. The piece is playful, bold, and unmistakably local — a love letter to a neighborhood that embodies Beijing's cosmopolitan energy.

The panda serves as both mascot and guide, leading the viewer through Wangjing's landmarks: the SOHO towers, Korean BBQ strips, tech campus courtyards, and the particular chaos of a neighborhood where startup culture meets immigrant communities.

The color palette is deliberately maximalist — a riot of oranges, pinks, teals, and golds that reflects the district's visual cacophony.

Wangjing Panda (望京熊猫): a neighborhood's soul captured in illustration.
